## THE BLOCKER (task #8) — read this before touching Track B again

Same policy weights, two feeds:

| fed | B rate | X rate | behavior |
|-----|--------|--------|----------|
| PARSED fixture states (offline) | 78.2% | 11.0% | **99.3% button agreement** — reproduces the fixture |
| LIVE bridge states | 100% | 0.1% | holds one reflector ~2000 frames, never shines |

The policy learned the correct function. It learned it in **parsed
coordinates**, and Peppi and libmelee disagree on `action_frame` — parsed
jumpsquat af 0,1,2 vs live 1,2,3, and the offset varies per action (365
agrees, 24/361/366 do not). "Release B at jumpsquat af 1,2" therefore never
fires live. Nothing in the training loss can reveal this: the loss is
computed entirely in parsed space. Full detail: GOTCHAS #81 (with the
escalation table) and the bottom of `MULTISHINE_DIAGNOSIS_2026-07-23.md`.

**First experiment — cheap and decisive.** Diff the two state streams on
the SAME game: the recorder's live observations (`MULTISHINE_TRACE=1` on
`record_multishine.exs`) against the parse of the .slp that run produced,
frame by frame, field by field. That gives the exact mapping (uniform +1 on
af? per-action? do other fields shift?). Only then pick a fix:

1. **Normalize at the embedding boundary** — one conversion, benefits every
   policy, but touches shared code; needs a Mewtwo regression pass, since
   coarse behaviors currently tolerate the shift and may quietly depend on it.
2. **Train drills on live-captured pairs** — have the recorder dump its own
   `(observed_state, decided_input)` stream; bypasses the replay round-trip
   entirely. Narrow and safe; only helps drills.
3. **Feed parsed-convention features live** — the inverse of (1).

**Do not spend more DAgger rounds on this.** DAgger fixes coverage gaps, not
feature mismatches; two rounds were spent proving that tonight.

### Two bugs found and fixed on the way (both would have recurred)

**State purity.** The first BC run floored at **loss 0.121 after 2000
epochs** instead of 2e-3. The recorder's fastfall stick-flick was keyed on
WALL-CLOCK parity over a 9-frame (odd) cycle, so identical states carried
different sticks on alternate cycles — BC was fitting a coin flip. The flick
was also vestigial: with the shine landing on airborne frame 1 Fox never
rises, so there is nothing to fastfall. Removed → **0.00148 in 12 epochs**
(167x fewer epochs). `inspect_multishine_table.exs` now checks state purity
so this is caught before a run is spent, not after.

**Truncated rollouts.** DAgger round 1 reported "0 frames, 0.0% corrected"
and silently trained on the fixture alone — plain BC wearing a DAgger label.
Slippi only finalizes a .slp on game END, and `analyze_policy_shine.exs`
just stopped. It now SDs out (`MULTISHINE_NO_SD=1` to skip).

**Bonus finding:** a stuck policy's rollout is ~2100 copies of ONE state. It
swamped the fixture and the policy collapsed from "never jump-cancels" to
"never shines". Added opt-in `--rollout-cap-per-state` to
`dagger_drill.exs` (caps frames per `{action, af, on_ground}`, full state
coverage preserved): 2609 → 514 frames. Default is unchanged behavior, so
the Mewtwo tracks are unaffected.

## New tooling (use these first next time)

- **`scripts/eval_policy_on_fixture.exs`** — offline (~10s, no Dolphin)
  discriminator: high agreement + live failure = state-stream problem; low
  agreement = training problem. **Run this before blaming exposure bias,
  DAgger coverage, or the bridge.** It is what cracked tonight's blocker.
- **`scripts/inspect_multishine_table.exs`** — table coverage, what each key
  presses, and the state-purity check. Its LIVE-fidelity number is computed
  in parsed space and does NOT predict live behavior (GOTCHAS #81) — treat
  as a hint; `demo_expert.exs` is authoritative.
- **`MULTISHINE_TRACE=1`** on `record_multishine.exs` — per-frame y/vy/
  action/af. First tool for any timing mystery; segment dumps cannot show
  physics.
- `analyze_policy_shine.exs` now always prints an action histogram, press
  rates and a frame sample, so a "no chains" run still says what happened.
