## Multishine (Track B) — the deepest open thread, do NOT reopen blind

Bradley watched the Fox multishine bot; it doesn't cleanly multishine. Full
chain of findings (all in `MULTISHINE_DIAGNOSIS_2026-07-23.md`), each proven:

1. **The metric was lying.** `ShineChain`'s first version conflated GROUNDED
   (act 360-363) and AERIAL (365-368) reflectors and reported the fixture as
   "max 73". Fixed: grounded-only chains + `grounded_fraction`. 14 tests.
2. **The fixture never had the behavior.** `fox_multishine_closed.slp` is
   **24.6% grounded** (275 ground / 844 air frames), max grounded chain **1**.
   The sloppy shine→jump→air-shine loop, not a multishine. Bradley called this
   on his first Fox message.
3. **Imitation was FAITHFUL** — bot ≈ teacher ≈ fixture. BC cloned bad data.
4. **Bridge latency = 0** (measured, `latency_probe.exs`). The "+1 latency" in
   old comments was wrong. **Latency compensation is RULED OUT.**
5. **The jumpsquat shine-cancel does NOT register through the libmelee pipe**
   (`jumpsquat_probe.exs`, exhaustive: held B / fresh edge / buffered X+B /
   open-loop rhythm sweep / JC-frame 0-4). Fox always takes off; the shine
   comes out in the AIR. Even buffering B from jumpsquat frame 1 fails.
6. **The trained policy doesn't multishine either** (`analyze_policy_shine.exs`)
   — it HOLDS a grounded reflector (~18 frames, X held = no jump edge) and
   re-enters it. Grounded *shining*, not multishine (max chain 1).

**The strategic conclusion:** no actor — script, teacher, or net — can produce
a jump-cancel grounded multishine through this bridge. Since inference uses the
SAME pipe, even a perfect human fixture won't let the bot EXECUTE it. **Track B
is a bridge-EXECUTION problem, above the fixture question.**

**Two paths (do NOT re-run the input sweeps — they're exhausted):**
- **(a) Fix the pipe frame-1 delivery** — why libmelee's pipe can't land a
  jumpsquat-frame shine-cancel (input-poll alignment / ExiAI-build quirk).
  This is the true unblock. Tools: `latency_probe.exs`, `jumpsquat_probe.exs`.
- **(b) Human-recorded fixture** (physical controller, normal Dolphin) for the
  DATA — but it only pays off once (a) works. HuggingFace
  slippi-public-dataset is the only source of real Fox multishines (local
  archive + B2 have none; best local grounded chain is 2, defensive shining).

New multishine tooling (all committed): `ShineChain` (+tests), `demo_expert.exs`
(drive a scripted teacher live), `demo_vs_teacher.exs` (policy P1 vs teacher
P2), `latency_probe.exs`, `jumpsquat_probe.exs`, `analyze_policy_shine.exs`,
`record_multishine.exs` now sweepable (`MULTISHINE_JC_FRAME/PERIOD/JUMP_AT/
SHINE2_AT`, `MULTISHINE_HEADLESS=1`, built-in port-2 dummy).
