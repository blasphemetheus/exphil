## The three facts that cracked it (don't re-litigate these)

1. **Down-B cannot cancel jumpsquat** (only up-smash/up-B/grab). The old
   probes/sweeps tested a nonexistent mechanic; the bridge was never at fault.
2. **A real multishine is ~half aerial.** Cycle (measured, 9 frames):
   grounded reflector x2 → jumpsquat x3 (af exactly 1..3) → AERIAL shine
   365 x3 + 366 x1 at y=0.0 → lands back into grounded reflector. ShineChain
   is now v3: chains continue through air gaps ≤8 frames that contain an
   aerial reflector; full jumps/shineless hops still break. Healthy
   grounded_fraction is **0.4–0.6** — never gate on ~1.0.
3. **The whole technique is one predictive frame.** Shine activating ON
   airborne frame 1 zeroes vy before the jump's +2.1 first-frame rise → Fox
   stalls at y≈0, lands in 4 frames. One frame later he's at y=2.1 and the
   aerial reflector's real physics (0.027/f² gravity — 8x weak, fastfall
   DISABLED in-state; all measured via `MULTISHINE_TRACE=1` per-frame y/vy)
   make it a 22-frame float. Reactive pressing is inherently 1 frame late;
   the teacher presses B on the OBSERVED last jumpsquat frame (af 3) so it
   arrives on airborne frame 1. B discipline: held through the entire
   reflector (releasing mid-air = 18-frame wind-down, kills the landing JC),
   released only on jumpsquat af 1-2 to arm the fresh edge.
