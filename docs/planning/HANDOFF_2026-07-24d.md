## 2. THE OPEN TECHNICAL PROBLEM (task #8)

**A replay-trained policy is not running on the features it was trained on.**
Same weights, two feeds:

| fed | B rate | X rate | behavior |
|-----|--------|--------|----------|
| PARSED fixture states (offline) | 78.2% | 11.0% | **99.3% button agreement** — reproduces the fixture |
| LIVE bridge states | 100% | 0.1% | holds one reflector ~2000 frames, never shines |

The policy learned the right function in **parsed coordinates**. Peppi and
libmelee disagree on `action_frame` — parsed jumpsquat af 0,1,2 vs live
1,2,3, and the offset varies per action (365 agrees; 24/361/366 do not). So
"release B at jumpsquat af 1,2" never fires live. The training loss cannot
reveal this: it is computed entirely in parsed space. Coarse behaviors
tolerate the shift, which is why it stayed invisible until a frame-perfect
technique had no slack for it — **so this plausibly affects every policy in
the repo, not just multishine.**

Full detail: GOTCHAS #81 (escalation table) and the last section of
`MULTISHINE_DIAGNOSIS_2026-07-23.md`.

**First experiment — cheap, decisive, needs `mix`.** Diff the two state
streams on the SAME game: the recorder's live observations
(`MULTISHINE_TRACE=1` on `record_multishine.exs`) against the parse of the
.slp that run produced, frame by frame, field by field. Output the exact
mapping: uniform +1 on af, or per-action, and whether any other field
shifts. Only then choose a fix:

1. **Normalize at the embedding boundary** — one conversion, benefits every
   policy. Touches shared code; needs a Mewtwo regression pass, because
   current behaviors may quietly depend on the shift. Probably right, but a
   real blast radius — Bradley's call, not a silent commit.
2. **Train drills on live-captured pairs** — recorder dumps its own
   `(observed_state, decided_input)` stream; bypasses the replay round-trip.
   Narrow and safe; helps drills only.
3. **Feed parsed-convention features live** — inverse of (1).

**Do not spend more DAgger rounds here.** DAgger fixes coverage gaps, not
feature mismatches; two rounds were spent proving that last night.
